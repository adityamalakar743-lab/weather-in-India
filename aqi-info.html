Air Quality Index & Safety Measures

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a scale from 1 to 5 that indicates how clean or polluted the air is. Understanding AQI helps you make informed decisions about outdoor activities.

1 Good

Air quality is satisfactory. Air pollution poses little or no risk. You can safely enjoy outdoor activities. Perfect for exercising, walking, and spending time outside. No special precautions needed.

2 Fair

Air quality is acceptable. However, there may be a risk for some people, particularly those who are unusually sensitive to air pollution. Most people can continue outdoor activities, but sensitive individuals should consider reducing prolonged outdoor exertion.

3 Moderate

Members of sensitive groups may experience health effects. The general public is less likely to be affected. Sensitive groups (children, elderly, people with heart or lung conditions) should reduce outdoor activities. Everyone else can continue normal activities but should watch for symptoms like coughing or shortness of breath.

4 Poor

Everyone may begin to experience health effects. Members of sensitive groups may experience more serious health effects. Everyone should reduce outdoor activities, especially strenuous exercise. Sensitive groups should avoid outdoor activities entirely. Consider wearing a mask if you must go outside.

5 Very Poor

Health alert: Everyone may experience serious health effects. Avoid all outdoor activities. Stay indoors as much as possible. Keep windows and doors closed. Use air purifiers if available. Sensitive groups should take extra precautions and consider staying indoors entirely. If you must go outside, wear a protective mask (N95 or better).
